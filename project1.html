KewBot

I had the amazing opportunity to be one of the eight students selected to represent Imperial College London during a five-week Summer School Program in Qingdao, China, at the Tsinghua Academy of Arts and Science Innovation Research (TASA). I had the privilege of participating in the first-ever International Design Education Laboratory (EduLab), organized by the International Council of Design and hosted by Tsinghua University.

This unique event brought together four top Chinese design schools and four international institutions, including Imperial, to collaborate on addressing the future 2050 challenges. Each team was tasked with imagining a "positive 2050," where responsible consumption prevails, or a "bleak 2050," where overconsumption continues. We had the opportunity to present to industry and government a solution to those 2050 challenges.

Our team was assigned the "good scenario," and we focused on KewBot, a health robot designed to enhance the well-being of elderly users through gardening. While it assists with gardening tasks, its primary goal is to improve both physical and mental health. KewBot adapts to each user’s needs, encouraging physical activity, reducing stress, and fostering social connections through community gardening. By integrating gardening into a health-focused routine, KewBot supports a healthier lifestyle, making it much more than just a gardening tool—it’s a health companion.

Matrix

The project began by envisioning our world in 2050. We developed a matrix to explore various future scenarios and quickly identified the development of green spaces, with a focus on the elderly, as the core area for our research.

Ideation

Our ideation process involved exploring diverse approaches to urban gardening and elderly wellness.

Prototyping

We developed prototypes to test and refine the concept.
